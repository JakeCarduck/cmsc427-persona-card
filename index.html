Aisha the Pragmatist and Community-Minded

Sophomore Biology Major

She hopes to go to med school. She is the president of her dorm and is involved in a community health outreach club.

Goals

  • A clear, separate section for 'Local Staff & Constitutent Services' with direct emails and areas of focus.
  • A public calendar of representatives' in-district events (i.e., town halls, office hours, community coffees).
  • Up-to-date info that avoids being pushing (such as trying to get donations for a campaign).

Frustrations

  • Figuring out which level of government is responsible for what; in other words, who do I need to visit to solve my particular problem?
    "The tools help you find who, but not which who for your specific problem."
  • Information overload and irrelevance.
    "Getting a list of 10 officials when I only need the one on the county health board. It can be paralyzing and make you give up."
  • Information is mixed together, making it hard to find the specific sections for the actual representatives vs. their public relations staff.
    "Why? Because that's how you actually build a relationship and get practical things done."
